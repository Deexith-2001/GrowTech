Wheat

Wheat is a staple crop grown across India. It thrives in cool, dry weather.

Step-by-step Cultivation:

  1. Plough and prepare the land during dry season.
  2. Select high-yield wheat varieties.
  3. Time sowing during October–November.
  4. Use appropriate irrigation at key growth stages.
  5. Apply fertilizers: NPK mix and compost.
  6. Manage weeds and monitor for pests.
  7. Harvest in March–April once grain hardens.

Rice

Rice is widely cultivated in water-rich regions. It requires standing water to grow.

Step-by-step Cultivation:

  1. Choose a fertile lowland field.
  2. Plough and puddle the land with water.
  3. Transplant seedlings from nursery after 20–30 days.
  4. Maintain standing water throughout growth.
  5. Fertilize during tillering and panicle formation stages.
  6. Control pests like stem borer and leaf folder.
  7. Harvest when grains are mature and golden yellow.
